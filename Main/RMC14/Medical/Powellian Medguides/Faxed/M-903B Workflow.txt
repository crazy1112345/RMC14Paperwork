[head=2]The function of Medbay[/head]
  The function of medbays of the 2nd Company differs significantly in that the patients are, by the nature of deployment, far away from the bay itself. Hence, bulk of the regular treatment is delegated to the HMs who deploy with the squads and the medbay functions in assisting them in the Procurement of meds, treatment of those that need more specialized facilities, & disposal of corpses.

────────────────────────────────────────

[head=2]Procurement of Meds[/head]
  [head=3]Chem Production[/head]
  Currently, the medbay is expected to be stocked of, by descending order of priority, Pures, Pills, & GC alongside their complementary goods by production.
  The pures refer to Mera & Derma, which are usually put on display at the line below the Lower chemstation in their own individual tanks. There are two empty tanks ops-start at the chemstation, which usually stores the Pures. A tank of pure chemical will usually not suffice and one will have to replenish it. However, if they were containing the same volume of chems, they would usually run out simultaneously. 
  A medbay is expected to have MB, KD, & FN+ Pills. Expect any HM would take 1~3 [mean=1.5~2?] Canisters of the former two and 1~2 [mean=1.1?] of FN+. They are to be stored in the Smart chemical storage of the Lower chemstation. For the production of FN+, one must grind Nutriment in an action called Nutrimining which, in summary, is the filling of tanks with nutriment ground from foodstuff. In many cases, 3ku ~ 5ku of Nutri would suffice for an ops. Consult the Auxiliary guide for details.
  All marines will expect the production of GC. Expect every single WS, SGO, & SLs will take GC, with their preferred form of storage being Reagent pouches with E-XS Pouch-injector. The pouch-injector sets must be produced in the Medilathe at the Lathe room. The recommended ratio is 2 sets to 1 tank of GC or with more leverage for the sets. GC is stored at the line above the HM Preps with the sets at anywhere in the lobby. However, do not expect that they will last the transit. It is preferrable to have a production of GC lest they take the Pures, putting an extra energy burden on Lower chem.
  As to how exactly might one produce, one is expected to read the Chems conventions & a dedicated chemguide.

  [head=3]Reqprep[/head]
  For the HMs cannot visit the shipside medbay easily post-deployment, the medbay is expected to reach them byself - preferably before the first supply drop occur. Currently, the preferred method for this is Req-prep: the delivery of meds en masse in a manageable manner; achieved by consolidating the same meds in a storage, usually a rig. A session of reqprep is usually in three phases: Basics, Pills, & GC.
  Basic req-prep involves the sending of Topicals: Both types of Kits, Basics; & Defibs with Epi vendor-injectors. They are packed in their own respective rigs & sent to req.
  Pills req-prep involves the sending of Pills: MB, KD, & FN+. Pack them in storages & send over. Might FN+ be expected to take long to produce, one could temporarily replace FN+ with bloodbags.
  GC req-prep is of sending Pressurized canisters of GC to req. Pressurized canisters are to be printed individually at a Medi-lathe. As these cannot be stored, send via crate.
  Req-prep is expected to be done as such req NEVER runs out of meds, implying it ought occur at-least once in an ops. A single session of req-prep at 14 of everything each (=1 Full-rig) will last them for about a hour or so. However, if they do run out of meds, med will NOT be notified more than half the time. Hence, it is recommended to be vigilant in checking req's stocks.

[head=2]Handling Medevacs[/head]
  If expected to be untenable or unable to treat a patient groundside, a HM will send them shipside by Medevac - an occurance a proper Flight crew would assuredly call out. If so, Medbay is expected to be ready to receive the medevac & treat them post-haste. It is good practice to wait at the expected hangar bay of arrival unless there already are sufficient quantity of crew on hold. As of now, there are two primary cases of medevac: Severe damage or xenonid infection (I.e. "Hugged").
  If a marine is that severely damaged, it usually is one of the three: Hellburns, Crated, or Fell from the boats. Regardless, however, one is simply expected to resuscitate and send them back down.
  A hugged patient will require surgery. One must drag the patient into the surgical bay and perform surgery - Any trained medifolk will instinctively know the proceedings once one makes an incision. A xenonid larvae will always reside in one's torso as the softer flesh of one's organs is its primary sustenance. As torso surgery requires the removal of armour, shaking a marine after unbagging them is good practice. 
  A nurse, however, cannot identify xeno larvae in flesh; for which they often are assistants and sometimes will prep. Prepping is the opening of ribcages & calling out for a surgeon. If a surgeon comes in the middle of prepping, it is recommended to vacate as soon as possible.

[head=2]Corpse Disposal[/head]
  The boats will also bring up corpses which are analysed by the shipside AI, the ASRS, automatically in its storage for Intel. Be the position of corpses obvious, a flight crew will not call it out.
  If the corpse is of a Humanoid, one must store the corpse in the Morgue. The ID is expected to be removed and put on the morgue for identification. Equipments of interest may also be removed and stockpiled to be sent to Req. Be the morgue "full", one is to stack the corpses as a morgue-unit can hold 30 bodies. One does NOT incinerate corpses unless ordered by the CMO.
  Xenonid corpses are stored in the Containment cells at Research. If one does not possess access for research, one may leave the corpse at Research's front door.
